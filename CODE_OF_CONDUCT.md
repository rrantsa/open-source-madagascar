# Code of Conduct

Open Source Madagascar aims to be a welcoming, respectful, and constructive community for contributors, maintainers, and readers.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that help build a healthy community include:

- showing respect in discussion and review;
- giving clear, honest, and constructive feedback;
- assuming good intent while still protecting quality and safety;
- welcoming contributors with different experience levels;
- keeping the focus on collaboration and shared learning.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in this repository or related spaces:

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks;
- hateful or discriminatory language;
- deliberate misrepresentation of projects or contributors;
- spam, scams, or malicious submissions;
- publication of private or sensitive information without permission;
- repeated disruptive behavior after being asked to stop.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

- issues;
- pull requests;
- review comments;
- discussions connected to the repository;
- other community interactions carried out in the name of the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers through a private communication channel when available. Include enough context for the maintainers to investigate fairly.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate to protect the community, including:

- asking for clarification or correction;
- editing or removing harmful content;
- closing disruptive discussions;
- rejecting malicious or unsafe submissions;
- temporarily or permanently restricting participation.

Enforcement decisions should aim to protect contributors, preserve trust, and keep the repository constructive.

## Project Expectation

This repository is intended to promote reuse, collaboration, and local digital progress. Community conduct should support that mission.
